Pelican vs Pterodactyl Panel (2026) – Which Should You Use?

⏱️ 6 min read 📅 Updated May 2026 ⚖️ Comparison

If you've been in the self-hosted game server world lately, you've probably heard about Pelican Panel — the community fork of Pterodactyl that's been gaining serious traction in 2025–2026. So which one should you actually use?

Short answer: it depends on what you're building. Here's the full breakdown.

Background

Pterodactyl Panel's original development slowed significantly after 2022. The core team became less active, leaving the community with an increasingly outdated codebase that didn't support modern PHP/Laravel versions.

In response, a group of community contributors forked the project and created Pelican Panel — a modernized, actively maintained rewrite that drops the old Wings daemon in favor of a new Rust-based backend called Pyrodactyl.

TL;DR: Pterodactyl is the original, stable, battle-tested panel. Pelican is the modern fork with active development but less community content (fewer eggs, guides, etc.) so far.

Which Should You Choose?

Choose Pterodactyl if...

  • You need a specific egg that doesn't exist for Pelican yet
  • You're following an existing tutorial or joining a community using Pterodactyl
  • You want maximum stability and don't want to deal with occasional rough edges
  • You're running a commercial hosting service that needs a proven base

Choose Pelican if...

  • You're starting fresh with no existing setup to maintain
  • You want a panel that will still be actively maintained in 2027+
  • You want the nicer, more modern UI for yourself or clients
  • You're comfortable with software that's still maturing

🏆 Our Verdict (2026)

For new setups: go with Pelican. The development momentum is clearly there, the UI is significantly better, and the egg compatibility is catching up fast.

For existing Pterodactyl setups: no need to migrate right now. Pterodactyl still works perfectly fine and the egg ecosystem is unmatched. Revisit migration in late 2026.
